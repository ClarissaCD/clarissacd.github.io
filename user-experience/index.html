VSCO Redesign

When I was learning about computer and user interaction, I redesigned the photography mobile app VSCO in an effort to improve its usability. One of the major issues I have with VSCO's interface is that its studio page edits pictures from your gallery unless you click through several buttons to make a copy to edit. In my redesign, you'd upload a picture from your gallery you can edit without harming the original.
The other issue that I resolved with the same screen was the placement of the camera icon in VSCO. In the original VSCO screen, the camera is in the upper right corner. In the redesign, the camera is accessible when you choose to add a picture to your studio. It looks cleaner on screen and is intuitive for mobile users.
The third issue I had with VSCO's original design was that its user search page and its image search were separate. Most platforms make searching their platform easy and use filters to differentiate users from images. I feel that VSCO already does a good job with its tabs as filters, so its separate search pages are not necessary. In my redesign, I combined the search pages.
